# Customer Segmentation Dashboard using RFM Analysis | Power BI

## Project Overview

This project presents an interactive Customer Segmentation Dashboard built in Power BI using the RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) framework. The dashboard transforms raw transactional sales data into meaningful business insights by identifying customer purchasing behavior and classifying customers into actionable segments.

The analysis enables businesses to recognize their most valuable customers, detect customers at risk of churn, monitor sales performance, and support data-driven marketing and customer retention strategies.

---

## Business Objective

The primary objective of this project is to answer key business questions such as:

* Who are the most valuable customers?
* Which customers purchase most frequently?
* Which customers generate the highest revenue?
* Which customers are at risk of becoming inactive?
* How do sales perform over time?
* Which products and countries contribute the most to revenue?

## RFM Analysis

The dashboard segments customers using three important metrics:

**Recency (R)** – Measures how recently a customer made a purchase.

**Frequency (F)** – Measures how often a customer makes purchases.

**Monetary (M)** – Measures the total amount spent by a customer.

Based on these metrics, customers are classified into segments such as:

* Champions
* Loyal Customers
* Potential Loyalists
* New Customers
* At Risk
* Lost Customers

This segmentation helps businesses design personalized marketing campaigns and improve customer retention.

## Interview Questions

### What is RFM Analysis?

RFM is a customer segmentation technique that evaluates customers based on how recently they purchased, how frequently they purchase, and how much they spend.

### Why is RFM important?

RFM helps businesses identify valuable customers, improve retention, optimize marketing campaigns, and increase customer lifetime value.

### Why did you use KPI Cards?

KPI cards provide an instant summary of the most important business metrics, allowing decision-makers to assess overall performance at a glance.

### Why did you use a Frequency vs Monetary Scatter Plot?

The scatter plot visualizes the relationship between purchase frequency and customer spending, making it easy to identify high-value customers, frequent buyers, and low-engagement customers.
